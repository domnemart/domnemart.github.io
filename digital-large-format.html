ever since i first saw an image through the ground glass of a medium-format camera, which seemed magical and astoundingly three-dimensional to me, i wanted to use that image for filmmaking. the opportunity came when i had the chance to shoot the film diorama directed by vida skerk.

the theme of the film is the fragility and unreliability of memory, so i conceived a visual concept that would emphasize this with its softness and extremely shallow depth of field. the setting of the film suggests the feeling of a diorama, a miniature, which could be somewhat achieved using a tilt-shift lens, but i wanted a more comprehensive approach. i made a device that acts as an adapter for a digital camera. for filming, i used a large-format industar 37 lens, and its image is projected onto a ground-glass-like surface, which is then captured by a digital camera. the large-format digital system allows for manual focusing during filming, as well as 'tilt' and 'shift' movements of the lens.
